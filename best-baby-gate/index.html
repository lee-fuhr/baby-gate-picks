Baby gates are an essential piece of safety gear once your little one starts crawling or walking. They keep curious explorers away from stairs, kitchens, and other off-limits areas, giving you peace of mind during a hectic season of life. But not all gates are created equal: some are better for stairways, others for doorways, and some sacrifice sturdiness for convenience. When choosing a gate, focus on where you’ll use it. For top and bottom of stairs, hardware-mounted gates are the only safe option because they screw into the wall. Pressure-mounted gates work fine between rooms but can be pushed over by an determined toddler. Also consider the width of your opening, the height of the gate, and whether you need a walk-through door or a pet-friendly pass-through. The right gate will fit your home, your child, and your daily routine.
