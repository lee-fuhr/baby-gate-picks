Baby gates are one of the first pieces of safety gear parents reach for once a little one starts crawling. They keep curious explorers away from stairs, kitchens, and other off‑limit zones without needing constant supervision. For budget‑minded families, the challenge is finding a gate that is both safe and affordable without sacrificing durability or ease of use. Two main mounting styles dominate the market: pressure‑mount gates, which install with tension and leave no wall damage, and hardware‑mount gates, which screw into the frame and are far more secure for use at the top of stairs. Budget gates typically lean toward pressure‑mount, but there are premium options that cost a bit more and deliver added peace of mind. The key is matching the gate to your doorway size, location, and how often you plan to open and close it.
